Payers and utilization managers publish the clinical policies they use during prior authorization reviews. Akomi automatically fetches the correct policy, provides you with a detailed and structured review of your progress notes against these policies, and gives you an extremely easy pathway to correcting the documentation.

## Selecting the Applicable Policy Pathway

Each policy contains distinct pathways—different reasons a patient may need a procedure, each with unique requirements. For example, a diagnostic epidural injection and a repeat therapeutic injection are separate pathways with different criteria. Akomi identifies all available pathways and selects the one matching the provider's clinical intent.

## Request for Information

When documentation gaps exist or the provider's intent needs clarification, Akomi generates a request for information. Anyone with context on the patient's care plan can respond.

<Check>
  Answering Akomi's request for information should take **less than 30
  seconds**.
</Check>

Requests are multiple choice questions that clarify the provider's intent or guide you toward meeting policy requirements. If your responses include _new_ information not in your EHR progress notes, Akomi may generate a Letter of Medical Necessity (with your approval) to document this and create a complete authorization package.

</Frame>

<Note>
  A request for information will often give you the option to withdraw an
  authorization request if you believe the patient doesn't meet the payers
  requirements. Doing so will terminate the request on Akomi.
</Note>

## Letter of Medical Necessity

Progress notes are the most common document submitted with authorization requests, but they're designed for continuity of care, not to justify medical necessity. This mismatch often leads to denials. A Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) explicitly explains why a procedure is medically necessary and should be covered. When your progress notes don't fully address the payer's criteria, Akomi generates an LMN to fill the gaps.
